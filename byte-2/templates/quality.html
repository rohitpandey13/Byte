Quality: About This Data Set

The data set is very dirty. At the beginning, it even does not contain headers. I manually explored the contents to identify most of the headers. Besides, most of movies only have its name, thus I chose a piece of data have most of the informations.

Is the data complete?

No. The missing of the necessary information such as duration, production company, poster, etc. may be due to the missing data of the source of the data collection (like some movie website). The missing of winners and nominees may be due to the fact that this movie did not have any winners or nominees.

Is the data coherent?

Some attributes are coherent, such as the movie name: always name + year, and the date: always in format YYYYMMDD. Meanwhile, the published dates (in readable format) match with the date attributes. If we explore the rationship between genre and rating, we can find that drama, thriller, romance and action/advance focus on R or NR; meanwhile Kid/Family focuses on PG or G and art/foreign focuses on NR. These relationship mathc my expectations and the distribution makes sense in some degree.

Is the data correct?

Actually, it is not easy to verify the correctness of the data. The only thing I can verify is the published date, because this information is duplicated in the data set, which is included in the name, datepublished and date. Basically, the date is matched. However, the other information is not easy to verify. You need to compare them with the online information to verify their correctness. Some attributes can be verified from their correlation, like the relationship between genre and rating.
